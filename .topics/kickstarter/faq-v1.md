# FAQ v1

## Is Tiinex open source?

Large parts of the ecosystem are already public and Git-based.

The long-term direction strongly favors:
- open formats
- inspectable artifacts
- portable structures
- recoverable workflows

---

## Is this blockchain?

No.

The project uses:
- Git
- lineage references
- checksums
- explicit artifacts

The goal is inspectability and recoverability, not decentralized finance.

---

## Is this AI memory?

Not in the traditional sense.

The focus is continuity infrastructure:
explicit traces, lineage, reconstruction, and recoverable collaboration.

---

## Why markdown?

Because:
- humans can read it
- Git handles it well
- it survives platforms
- it avoids vendor lock-in
- it remains future-proof

---

## Why Kickstarter?

The Kickstarter exists to:
- validate public interest
- collect real-world feedback
- fund development time
- support tooling stabilization
- improve documentation and public infrastructure

---

## Is this trying to replace Git?

No.

Git is treated as a foundational lineage layer, not a problem to eliminate.

---

## Is this only for developers?

No.

The long-term goal is broader:
- researchers
- creators
- teams
- communities
- AI-assisted workflows
- recoverable public collaboration

---

## What happens if the creator disappears?

The structure is intentionally designed around portability and inspectability.

The goal is to reduce dependency on any single runtime or individual.

---

## Is everything public?

No.

The project explicitly supports:
- omitted data
- redacted information
- selective disclosure
- recoverable but bounded continuity

---

## What does “owned continuity” mean?

It means:
- you control your artifacts
- you control your exports
- you decide what persists
- you decide what gets shared
- continuity should not depend on hidden vendor systems
